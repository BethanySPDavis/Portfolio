  • What is it?

    • Simply, a copy of the Channel 4 show “Taskmaster”, following as much of their formatting as I could manage without access to the original music files or decor, and a group of friends rather than celebrities. Unlike the existing lockdown taskmaster, whereby Alex Horne would issue tasks via social media and evaluate video submissions, and following on from a series of Zoom quizzes which I couldn’t contribute to in a meaningful sense due to not quite understanding the levels I should pitch the questions at, I decided I’d write, direct, edit and present an episode of taskmaster to be completed at home. While my show omitted the role of an assistant, having me fill in the Alex Horne and Greg Davies roles, it kept to other more familiar parts of the show, such as the grouping of competitors based on approach to task, the use of thematic music, and the editing down of tasks into short digestible segments, rather than showing entries in full. Finally, once I’d finished recording and editing the tasks, I presented the show with a power point presentation to manage the clips which I was displaying with Zoom's screen share feature, and breaks in between for what I hoped were amusing ad lib comments, with contributions from the participants.

  • Why I made it

    • As mentioned previously, I found it difficult when it was my turn to produce a quiz for weekly meetings due to a lack of ability to pitch the sorts of questions my friends would know anything about, and rather than use that as an excuse to not do anything, I decided it’d be better to involve everyone that wanted in this project. As well as just being fun to watch my friends attempt tasks I’d deliberately written to have methods of “cheating” through clever reading between the lines, it was also a different challenge from anything I’d done before, effectively taking the role of a leader on a project, managing every aspect, while also having a crew of people to manage, as while nobody else contributed to the editing or writing (by choice, I didn’t want them to have an early look at the tasks or results), it was still work on their part creating the required amusing parts of the videos. While I learned a lot about how each of them approach the task of constructing small towers with inadequate building materials, I also learned a great deal more about managing a fairly large scale project with multiple contributors, and had a fantastic time doing it.
